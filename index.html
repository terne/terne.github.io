Intro

Since 2023, I have been a postdoctoral researcher at the Copenhagen Research Center for Biological and Precision Psychiatry and the Novo Nordisk Foundation Center for Metabolic Research. I am currently working on the PRECIS-EHR project, funded by The Lundbeck Foundation and the Mental Health Services in the Capitol Region of Denmark. Within the PRECIS-EHR project, I am developing NLP methods for analysing clinical notes, at a large scale, and developing Large Language Models for predicting psychiatric outcomes.

My main research area is Natural Language Processing (NLP), with a focus on evaluation methods and recognising biases of NLP models. I hold a PhD in Social Data Science from the University of Copenhagen (2023). My PhD thesis was on "Biases in Natural Language Processing" and consisted of highly interdisciplinary studies conducted at the Copenhagen Center for Social Data Science (SODAS) and the CoAStaL NLP group.

In my spare time I like to play Dungeons & Dragons and board games, drink lots of coffee, and pet cats when the opportunity arises.

  • 2020 - 2021 Introduction to Social Data Science (Master’s level course), University of Copenhagen.
    Lecturer on the subject “Text as Data” in two consecutive years. The course targeted students with little or no experience with Python and my lectures offered a practical introduction to pre- processing text data and performing a simple text classification task, taught with a mixture of exercises, recorded video material, live lecture and office hours. My video material is publicly available on YouTube.
  • 2020 - 2021 Student supervision, University of Copenhagen.
    Co-supervisor for two MSc theses and supervisor for two independent projects, all of students from the Department of Economics applying NLP methods to economic research questions.
  • 2021 Advanced Social Data Science II (Master’s level course), University of Copenhagen.
    Teaching Assistant. The course taught advanced, practical Python Machine/Deep Learning and Topic Modelling with, especially, images and text data.
  • 2020 Social Data Science: Text as Data and Deep Learning (Master’s level course), University of Copenhagen.
    Teaching Assistant. The course introduced students to Deep Learning with theoretical foundations and with practical Keras exercises. The course had an emphasis on text data but included exercises with images as well.
  • 2018 - 2019 Scientific Programming (Master’s level course), University of Copenhagen.
    Teaching Assistant. The course offered a practical introduction to Machine Learning and data analysis with Python, using both structured and unstructured data.
  • 2019 Humanistiske Designmetoder II (Bachelor’s level course), Roskilde University.
    Guest Lecturer on the subject “Nudging” and research design methods relevant for nudging experiments.
